Mental Health

Eating Habits

    Eating habits represent a significant factor in an individual’s mental health, including healthy and consistent eating. Teenagers who frequently eat “low-quality diets” are 80% more likely to suffer from mental health issues, including depression and anxiety. Healthy eating, however, regularly leads to better patient scores in mental health examinations done by professionals. It is important to incorporate at least one fruit and one vegetable into each meal you eat. Also, avoiding fried, grilled, or high-fat foods is essential.

Excercise

    Physical activity such as jogging, swimming, walking, and dancing has been shown to be helpful in improving mental health by lowering depression and anxiety. Exercise can also aid in relieving low self-esteem and social withdrawal. These health benefits can be taken a dvantage of by exercising thirty minutes, continuously or non-continuously, per day, three days a week.
